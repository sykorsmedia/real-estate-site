FREQUENTLY ASKED QUESTIONS

Have questions? We have answers! Check out our FAQ section to find useful information about our real estate services and process.

1. How can I properly value my property before I put it up for sale?

To properly assess the value of your property before selling it, it is important to analyze similar properties in the area and consider the overall condition of the home. A professional appraisal from a certified appraiser can provide an objective estimate, and consulting with local real estate agents will give you valuable information about market trends.

2. What are the steps to buy a property through the agency?

Typically: define your budget and preferences, view shortlisted properties, make an offer, complete due diligence and financing, then sign contracts and close. We guide you at each step and coordinate with lenders and legal counsel as needed.

3. What documents are required to conclude a real estate transaction?

Common requirements include proof of identity, title and survey documents, disclosure forms, purchase agreement, and lender paperwork when a mortgage is involved. We provide a checklist specific to your sale or purchase.

4. Does the agency offer rental property management services?

Yes. We can help market your rental, screen tenants, prepare leases, and coordinate ongoing management tasks so landlords and tenants have a smooth experience.

5. How can I schedule a viewing of a property?

Call us, email, or use the contact form on this page with the property reference and your preferred times. We will confirm an appointment and send any access details you need.

6. What commission does the agency charge for brokerage services?

Commission structures depend on the type of transaction and local market practice. We explain fees upfront in writing so there are no surprises before you sign a representation agreement.
